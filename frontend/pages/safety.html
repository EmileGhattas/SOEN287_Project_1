Laboratory Safety Code

Your guide to safe and responsible laboratory practices.

General Overview

Laboratory safety is everyoneâ€™s responsibility. All users must be familiar with the safety code before working in any lab. Following these rules ensures a secure, efficient, and productive environment for all.

1. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

  • Always wear a lab coat, safety goggles, and gloves.
  • Tie back long hair and avoid loose clothing or jewelry.
  • Closed-toe shoes are mandatory at all times.

2. Conduct and Behavior

  • No eating, drinking, or chewing gum in the laboratory.
  • Never work alone: always have a supervisor or partner present.
  • Keep your workspace clean and uncluttered.
  • Label and organize all containers, samples, and chemicals properly.

3. Chemical Safety

  • Always read and understand the Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS) before using any substance.
  • Use fume hoods for volatile or hazardous materials.
  • Never return unused chemicals to their original containers.
  • Dispose of chemical waste only in labeled, approved bins.

4. Equipment Use

  • Inspect all equipment before use and report any damage immediately.
  • Turn off and unplug devices when finished.
  • Use protective shields for reactions under pressure or high temperature.

5. Emergency Procedures

  • Know the location of fire extinguishers, eyewash stations, and first aid kits.
  • Report all accidents, spills, or injuries immediately to the supervisor.
  • Evacuate calmly if an alarm sounds and follow posted exit routes.

6. Waste Disposal

  • Separate chemical, biological, and sharps waste.
  • Use approved containers and label them clearly.
  • Do not overfill bins or leave open waste containers unattended.

7. After the Experiment

  • Clean all glassware and tools before storing them.
  • Wipe down benches with disinfectant or approved cleaner.
  • Sign out and confirm your lab area has passed post-use inspection.

Final Note

Safety is the foundation of all successful laboratory work. By following these protocols, you protect yourself, your peers, and your research. Always be alert, responsible, and respectful of the shared lab space.

Violation of these rules may result in suspension of lab access privileges.
